AP Course Structure at Tutela

Diagnostic & Onboarding

  • A baseline diagnostic test pinpoints subject knowledge, strengths, and areas for improvement.
  • Mentors analyze results and create a personalized study plan mapped to the syllabus of the units and skills (conceptual understanding + free-response practice) of the selected AP Subject.
  • Parents and students receive a customized roadmap with timelines, milestones, and goals before classes begin.

Concept & Strategy Sessions

  • Duration: 12–24 weeks (customized by subject and student pace).
  • Course Content Delivery: Aligned to the College Board’s CEDs across subjects in STEM, Humanities, and Social Sciences.
  • Test Strategy Training:
    • Time management for multiple-choice and free-response questions.
    • Essay structuring, data interpretation, and problem-solving techniques.
  • Practice Resources: 50,000+ practice questions across courses on Tutela Connect, supplemented with past exams and official College Board samples.

Continuous Assessment

  • Course Progress Tests: Monthly assessments to benchmark performance against AP standards and exam format.
  • Sectional Tests: Unit-wise testing for mastery, with analytics on pacing, accuracy, and question-type performance.
  • Reviews: Sessions with experts after every test to refine strategy, clarify mistakes, and strengthen weak areas.

Simulated AP Full-Length Mock Tests

  • 30–40 full-length AP mocks per subject, including past papers, replicating the latest digital / hybrid exam formats (Bluebook-style) and timing.
  • Builds exam stamina, confidence, and time control under real AP conditions.
  • Post-test debriefs with mentors ensure targeted revision and score stability.
  • Revision Loops: Focused revision sessions of recurring weak areas to achieve 4+ performance.

How long is the typical AP prep course?

Most students follow a 12-24 week customized program that runs from January/February to the May exams. Shorter intensive tracks and year-round options are available for students starting late or adding multiple APs.

When should my child start AP exam preparation?

The ideal time is late sophomore year or early junior year (January-March) for May exams. Starting early gives time for deep concept building, multiple practice exams, and FRQ confidence without last-minute stress.

Is Tutela’s AP coaching truly one-on-one?

Yes - every student receives private one-on-one online sessions (or small groups of up to 4 students at our centers). Lessons, homework, mocks, and FRQ reviews are 100% tailored to your child’s school curriculum, strengths, and target colleges.

What kind of score improvement can we expect?

Our curriculum is fully updated for the College Board’s latest formats (Bluebook platform, digital submissions, and hybrid FRQs). Students practice daily on the exact interface they’ll see in May, including timed digital FRQs and adaptive question sets.

What practice resources are included in the AP prep courses?

Unlimited access to 50,000+ College Board-aligned questions, 10-15 full-length proctored mocks per subject, hundreds of FRQ prompts with model answers, and detailed score reports after every test — all reviewed one-on-one with an expert tutor.
